CARCINOGENICITY & TOXICITY OF AROMATIC HYDROCARBONS

Topics covered today:
9.6 Carcinogenicity and Toxicity

✓ SESSION COMPLETE

KEY CONCEPTS FROM TODAY:
  • Benzene and PAHs are chemically stable but are metabolized into reactive, DNA-damaging intermediates
  • PAHs form especially during incomplete combustion (smoke, exhaust, charred food)
  • Carcinogenicity arises from metabolic activation, not direct reactivity of the parent molecule
  • Minimizing combustion byproduct exposure reduces associated cancer risk
