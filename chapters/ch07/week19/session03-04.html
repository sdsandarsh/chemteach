✓ SESSION COMPLETE

KEY CONCEPTS FROM TODAY:
  • Ka (acid ionization constant) and Kb (base ionization constant) measure ionization strength quantitatively
  • Larger Ka/Kb means stronger acid/base, more ionization at equilibrium
  • Strong acids/bases ionize ~100% and don't have a meaningful Ka/Kb
  • Ka × Kb = Kw = 10^-14 for any conjugate acid-base pair at 25°C
