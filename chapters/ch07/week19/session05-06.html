6.11.6-6.11.7 Polybasic Acids

KEY CONCEPTS FROM TODAY:
  • Polybasic acids ionize in sequential steps, each with its own Ka (Ka1, Ka2, Ka3...)
  • Each successive ionization constant is progressively smaller (weaker)
  • This happens because removing H+ from an increasingly negative ion is progressively harder
  • Ka1 alone usually dominates pH calculations for polybasic acids
