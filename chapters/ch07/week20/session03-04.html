6.11.9 Hydrolysis of Salts

✓ SESSION COMPLETE

KEY CONCEPTS FROM TODAY:
  • Hydrolysis: a salt's ions react with water, affecting solution pH
  • Salt of weak acid + strong base → basic solution (e.g. sodium acetate)
  • Salt of strong acid + weak base → acidic solution (e.g. ammonium chloride)
  • Salt of strong acid + strong base → neutral, no hydrolysis (e.g. sodium chloride)
