Topics covered today:
8.1-8.3 Tetravalency of Carbon, Structural Representations

✓ SESSION COMPLETE

KEY CONCEPTS FROM TODAY:
  • Carbon's tetravalency (always 4 bonds) is the foundation of organic chemistry's vast diversity
  • Carbon's catenation ability lets it form long chains, branches, and rings with itself
  • Structural representations: molecular formula, Lewis structure, condensed formula, skeletal formula
  • Skeletal formulas imply carbon and hydrogen atoms based on the fixed tetravalency rule
