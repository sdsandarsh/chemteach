2.5.2 Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

✓ SESSION COMPLETE

KEY CONCEPTS FROM TODAY:
  • Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle: Δx · Δp ≥ h/4π
  • Position and momentum of a particle cannot both be known with perfect precision simultaneously
  • This is a fundamental property of nature, not a measurement flaw
  • It directly disproves Bohr's fixed-orbit model and justifies the orbital (probability) picture
