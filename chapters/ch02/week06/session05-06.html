2.4.1 Limitations of Bohr's Model

✓ SESSION COMPLETE

KEY CONCEPTS FROM TODAY:
  • Bohr's formulas: rn = 0.529(n²/Z) Å, En = -13.6(Z²/n²) eV — valid for hydrogen-like atoms only
  • Limitation 1: fails for multi-electron atoms (ignores electron-electron repulsion)
  • Limitation 2: cannot explain Zeeman effect or Stark effect (spectral line splitting)
  • Limitation 3: contradicts wave nature of electrons and Heisenberg's uncertainty principle
  • These gaps motivated the quantum mechanical model
